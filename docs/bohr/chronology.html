Chronological overview

1885: Niels Bohr born — Strandgade in Copenhagen. His family moves shortly thereafter to the medical institute on Bredgade, where Niels’ father, Christian, has been appointed professor of physiology

1913: Bohr publishes three papers about the structure of atoms

1926

  • October: Schrödinger visits Copenhagen. What happened during this visit has become the subject of some controversy — especially with ad hominem accusations that Bohr bullied Schrödinger. (So what if Bohr did bully Schrödinger? Does that give us reason to think less of Bohr’s views? We suspect that the reason people have been intent on showing that Bohr bullied Schrödinger is because they want to show that Bohr’s views caught on not because they were persuasive, but because Bohr used non-rational methods of persuasion.)
