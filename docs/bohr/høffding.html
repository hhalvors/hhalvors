Harald Høffding

Harald Høffding’s life spans a time of momentous change in Europe as a whole, and in Denmark in particular. On February 20, 1843, Kierkegaard’s first book Either-Or was published. Høffding was born less than a month later, on March 11th. When Kierkegaard died on November 11, 1855, Høffding was twelve years old. It was a mere five years later when Høffding began his studies at the University of Copenhagen, where he came under the influence of Rasmus Nielsen.

Høffding died in 1931, when Niels Bohr was forty-six years old, and almost twenty years after the publication of Bohr’s groundbreaking trilogy on the quantum atom. (Høffding’s lifespan runs closely parallel to that of Georg Brandes (1842-1927).)

Høffding wrote several topical works later in his life, and these give his considered opinion on matters of epistemology, ethics, etc. Høffding offers less in the way of opinion about metaphysical matters. We offer some selected commentary below, although these works cover a lot more than we mention here.
