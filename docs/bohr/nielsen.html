Hans Halvorson Physics, Logic, Philosophy

Rasmus Nielsen

During his lifetime, Rasmus Nielsen (1809-1884) was the most renowned philosopher in Scandinavia. Unfortunately for his philosophical legacy, he was an explicitly Christian philosopher (most interersted in finding place for both faith and reason) at the time that the Scandinavian countries were secularizing. In short, the next generation of Scandinavian thinkers simply had no use for Nielsen’s philosophy, and so it has been almost completely forgotten. (Not a single one of Nielsen’s philosophical works has been translated into English.)

We believe that Rasmus Nielsen is the key figure for understanding Niels Bohr’s relationship to the philosophical tradition. While Nielsen was explicitly theological, he was struggling fiercely to incorporate knowledge from the empirical and mathematical sciences. In one sense, Nielsen was the first dedicated philosopher of science in European history.

Open questions

There is much work to be done in figuring out what Rasmus Nielsen said — and evaluating whether any of it is worthy of our consideration. Besides his many published works (all in Danish), Nielsen left handwritten notes that have been preserved by the Royal Library of Denmark. We also have notes written by his students, e.g. Harald Høffding.
