Niels Bohr: Philosopher in Action

Niels Bohr wasn’t a professional philosopher, but he was an exemplar of a professional scientist who also wore the hat of a philosopher. In particular, Bohr’s work in physics was driven by philosophical vision; and the outputs of his research provided material for continuing philosophical reflection. For example, Bohr frequently spoke of “the epistemological lesson of quantum physics”. Whether or not we agree with Bohr about what the philosophical lessons of quantum physics are, I think we can all admire the attempt to bring one’s overall worldview into harmony with the deliverances of science. In short, Bohr had a “pro-science attitude” and a philosophy of life to support that attitude.

While we wish to understand what Bohr’s “philosophy” was, we don’t think it’s helpful to try to shove him into the classification schemes that analytic philosophers have created. For example, there has been an argument about whether Bohr is a “antirealist” or a “realist”. However, there isn’t any consensus about what it means to be an antirealist or realist, and so it is otiose to try to figure out which box Bohr belongs in.
